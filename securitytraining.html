Security Training

Scroll

1.Lockdown training

This course will provide participants with knowledge on:
• Incidents requiring a lockdown
• Types of Lockdown
• Stages of Lockdown
• Communication during a Lockdown- Internal & External

Duration : 2 - 2 ½ hours
Batch Size : Maximum 35
Pre-Requisites : None
Recommended for : Emergency Response Team and Administration

2.Security Guards training

This course equips the participants with the skills of guarding, patrolling, incident reporting, communication, behavioral and grooming skills.

Duration : 14 hours
Batch Size : Maximum 35
Pre-Requisites : None
Recommended for : Security Guard

3. Incident Investigation

• This course imparts the basics of an effective incident investigation.
• It enhances the situational analysis skills to conduct incident analysis for various case studies.

Duration : 8 hours
Batch Size : Maximum 25
Pre-Requisites : None
Recommended for : Security Guards and Administration

4.Close Protection Officer Training

Duration : 40 hours
Batch Size : Maximum 10
Pre-Requisites : None
Recommended for : Security

• This training is targeted to provide participants with the necessary knowledge to be engaged in Close protection operations.
• The course involves theoretical strategizing sessions & practical scenario based simulations to ensure optimum applicability.

5. Terror / Hostage Situations

Duration : 2 hours
Batch Size : Maximum 50
Pre-Requisites : None
Recommended for : All Employees

This course will provide participants with information on various hostile situations. Key elements of this training include: br
• Guidelines on how to react and respond in such situations
• Various psychological syndromes associated with them
• Response actions during an active shooter

6.Bomb Threat & Terror Situations

Duration : 2 hours
Batch Size : Maximum 20
Pre-Requisites : None
Recommended for : Security and administration

In this course, participants are able to:
• Understand types of Bomb threats
• Deal with unidentified objects
• Record information received during a potential bomb threat
• IEDs- Dangers and Management
• Develop a bomb incident plan
• Carry out initial & secondary searches
• Guidelines on how to react and respond in such situations- NITS briefing, HOT principle etc.
