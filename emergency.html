Emergency Response Training

SKIPL Basic First Aid (Awareness)

This training program on First aid and CPR signifies the first and immediate assistance given to any person suffering from a serious illness or injury, with care provided to preserve life, prevent the condition from worsening and to promote recovery.

Duration : 4 hours
Batch Size : 50
Pre-Requisites : None
Recommended for : All

Basic Fire Fighting - Theory + Practical’s

Duration : 3 hours
Batch Size : Maximum 30
Pre-Requisites : None
Recommended for : All Employees

The purpose of Basic firefighting training is to give participants skills, knowledge and expertise that will enable them to identify the conditions capable of causing fire, know how to use a fire extinguisher, respond appropriately to fire emergencies and follow the fire evacuation plan, adequately implement fire emergency procedures.
    Upon completion of this course participants will be able to:
  • Understand Fire chemistry
  • Understand Classification of fire
  • Understand The behavior of fire during its propagation
  • Successfully extinguish fires with the use of a fire extinguisher
  • Successfully evacuate during fire
  • Carry out a simple fire risk assessment on the premises.

Advanced Fire Fighting - Hydrants and hoses

Duration : 4 hours
Batch Size : Maximum 25
Pre-Requisites : Must have completed MI Basic Fire
Fighting course within 3 months (or could do the Basic Fire Fighting course with this course on the same day)
Recommended for : Emergency Response Team

The advanced course is designed for those designated to control firefighting operations on board with particular attention paid to organisation, tactics and command. The course will involve both practical and theory training.

    Upon completion of this course participants will be able to:
  • In depth detail fire extinguisher
  • Flashover and backdraft
  • Transfer of heat
  • PPE for fire fighter
  • Detection system
  • Fire pane
  • Sprinkler system
  • Use of Fire Hydrant system
  • Fire hose and fittings
  • Rescue drag and carry techniques
